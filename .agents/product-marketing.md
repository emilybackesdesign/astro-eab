## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Hiring teams and clients can't easily find a product designer who combines genuine UX research depth, strong UI craft, AND the operational/people skills to navigate complex stakeholders and messy requirements.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Many portfolios show polished UI but thin process and no measurable outcomes
- Junior/bootcamp portfolios lack real business impact and stakeholder experience
- Pure-visual designers struggle with research, ambiguity, and process
**What it costs them:** Bad hires/engagements are expensive — wasted ramp time, redesigns, and products that miss the real user/business need.
**Emotional tension:** Fear of being misled by a slick portfolio; doubt about whether a designer can operate independently and lead through ambiguity.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Other product/UX designers' portfolios — many over-index on visuals and under-deliver on outcomes and process.
**Secondary:** LinkedIn profiles, Dribbble/Behance pages — show work but little narrative, reasoning, or measurable impact.
**Indirect:** Referrals/network hires and design agencies — lower perceived risk, but less tailored and often more expensive than the right individual.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Unusual blend: 8 yrs technical recruiting (interviewing/listening) + 7 yrs gov-contracting ops (prioritize, simplify, automate) + formal UX/UI design
- Outcome-driven case studies with real numbers (~$100k/yr returning revenue, doubled signups in 3 months, patient scheduling cut in half, replaced ZenDesk with an AI chatbot)
- Strong at the hard parts: requirement gathering, stakeholder management, leading through ambiguity
- **A genuinely distinctive portfolio** — recruiters and hiring managers consistently call it unique, well-crafted, and a style they rarely see, in a field where most portfolios are bland. The site itself is proof of craft.
- **Healthcare software domain experience** — currently designing healthcare UX/UI; understands the complex workflows, stakeholders, and constraints of health-tech (reinforced by the Folsom Psychology patient-scheduling case study).
**How we do it differently:** Leads with research and synthesis, ties every design decision to a user/business outcome, and brings an operator's instinct for simplifying broken processes.
**Why that's better:** Lower hiring risk — a designer who can talk to users, manage stakeholders, and ship measurable impact.
**Why customers choose us:** They get craft + research + operational maturity + a person teams genuinely like working with.

**Pull:** A standout, well-crafted portfolio + proof of measurable outcomes + research depth + a likable, low-drama collaborator.
**Habit:** Defaulting to referrals, agencies, or "safe" pedigreed candidates.
**Anxiety:** "Can a career-changer operate at the level we need?" / "Is the personality a culture fit or a distraction?"
