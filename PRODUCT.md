# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, design leads, startup founders, and freelance clients evaluating Emily for a senior product design role or collaboration. The audience is broad — anyone who might hire or work with her — and arrives skeptical or curious. They need to leave convinced of both her craft and her judgment. The portfolio serves multiple conversion paths simultaneously: full-time job applications, inbound freelance leads, and general professional reputation.

## Product Purpose

Demonstrate that Emily Backes is a rigorous, experienced product designer who is also a person worth working with. The portfolio earns trust through case study depth and earns warmth through personality. Neither leg stands without the other: strip the personality and it's just another portfolio; strip the rigor and the puns are noise.

Success looks like: the right people reach out, and the wrong fits self-select out before wasting anyone's time.

## Brand Personality

Serious work, light spirit. Expert, direct, and self-aware. The cat puns aren't decoration — they're a deliberate filter. Readers who appreciate the playfulness are the right fit; readers who don't have learned something useful too.

Three words: **witty, rigorous, human**.

## Anti-references

- **Generic Notion-aesthetic portfolios** — ultra-minimal, beige or white, gridless "design thinking" vibes. Personality-free, indistinguishable.
- **Safe corporate portfolios** — polished but bland. Looks like every other senior designer's site. Could be anyone.
- **Dribbble-pretty-but-shallow** — heavy on visual polish, light on process and thinking. Impresses at a glance, disappears under scrutiny.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach** — The portfolio is itself a UX artifact. Every scroll, transition, copy choice, and information hierarchy proves the designer's skill. The medium is part of the message.
2. **Earn the puns** — The playful framing only works because the case studies behind it are rigorous. Humor is the reward for scrolling, not the hook to get people started.
3. **Broad signal, specific proof** — The portfolio speaks to many audience types, but every case study satisfies the most skeptical reader. Breadth of appeal, depth of evidence.
4. **Show, don't tell** — Personality lives in the structure, copy rhythm, and interaction details — not in "About Me" declarations. A reader should feel who Emily is before she's said it directly.
5. **The cat stays in the brand** — The feline motif is specific, owned, and differentiating. Not decoration but a through-line: it signals confidence, humor, and intentionality all at once.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA as baseline. The dark-first palette must maintain ≥4.5:1 contrast for all body text. Reduced-motion parity is non-optional throughout — every animation needs a crossfade or instant fallback. No assumptions about ambient light or display type.
