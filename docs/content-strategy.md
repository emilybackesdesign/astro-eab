# Content Strategy — Emily Backes Portfolio
*Last updated: 2026-06-20*

---

## Situation

**Goal:** Scale from ~500 impressions/day → 10,000 impressions/day (and ~10 → ~100 clicks/day).
**Lever:** Indexed, ranking content. CTR is already healthy (~2%); the bottleneck is reach.
**Constraint:** Two audiences with different needs that require different content — but both are served by Emily being genuinely useful and non-bland.

**Two audiences:**
| Audience | Primary job | Content goal |
|---|---|---|
| Hiring (primary) | Evaluate Emily as a senior product designer | Signal depth, seniority, domain expertise |
| Peer designers (secondary) | Get practical help for their own work | Be genuinely useful; drive return traffic + sharing |

**The current content gap:**
The 12 existing posts are strong for junior/early-career peer designers (portfolio advice, how-to UX research, career journey). What's missing:
- **Senior-level signals** — content that shows product-thinking depth, not just execution skills
- **Healthcare/health-tech** — Emily's real differentiator, almost entirely untapped
- **Stakeholder + process content** — her unusual background; what makes her stand out
- **Free tools** — high-intent, high-shareability, and currently zero
- **"Product Designer" positioning** — she's crossed that threshold; content should reflect it

---

## Content Pillars

### Pillar 1 — The Product Designer's Craft
*"The practical, process-deep content peer designers actually need"*

Covers: UX research depth, stakeholder management, requirements, design systems, and the operational side of design that textbooks skip.

**Why this pillar:** It serves the peer audience (traffic engine) AND signals seniority to the hiring audience. Posts here let Emily showcase her operator's instinct — not just "how to wireframe" but "how to align five stakeholders on the wrong direction."

**Connects to product:** Emily's differentiator is combining 8 yrs recruiting + 7 yrs ops + design — this is where that shows.

---

### Pillar 2 — Healthcare UX & Health-Tech Design
*"The niche authority play nobody else is owning"*

Covers: What makes health-tech UX different, clinical workflows, patient-facing vs. clinician-facing design, EHR integration patterns, outcomes measurement.

**Why this pillar:** Emily is actively designing healthcare software — she has first-hand expertise almost no design bloggers have. Competition in this search space is sparse. It's also the most direct signal to her target hiring audience (health-tech companies).

**Connects to product:** Folsom Psychology case study (scheduling cut in half) is the anchor proof point here.

---

### Pillar 3 — Career & Portfolio: The Senior Product Designer Level-Up
*"The transition from 'UX Designer' to 'Product Designer' — and what that actually means"*

Covers: The UX → Product Designer title/scope shift, portfolio strategy for senior roles, what hiring managers look for, job search for experienced designers.

**Why this pillar:** Massive search volume on "product designer vs UX designer" and related terms. Serves peer designers who are mid-career. Also lets Emily speak credibly to the hiring audience about what she signals — and why it matters.

**Connects to product:** Emily made this exact transition. She can write from lived experience, not theory.

---

### Pillar 4 — AI in Product Design
*"Designing AI products — from someone who actually built one"*

Covers: UX for AI features and chatbots, AI tools for designers, designing for uncertainty, the honest take on AI replacing design work.

**Why this pillar:** High search volume and social sharing right now. Emily has real credibility (she designed Sage, an LLM chatbot that replaced ZenDesk). Doesn't require healthcare domain but pairs with it naturally.

**Connects to product:** Sage case study is the anchor. "How to UX an AI chatbot" is a direct path from the case study to a high-ranking post.

## Publishing Cadence (Recommended)

Emily is a solo creator with a day job. Suggested cadence: **2 posts/month** with quality prioritized over volume.

**Month 1–2:** Tier 1 anchors (posts #1–4 above). These are the hub posts that everything else links to.
**Month 3–4:** Tier 2 posts building out clusters. Start first free tool.
**Month 5+:** Fill cluster spokes. Second free tool. Revisit Pillar 2 with more healthcare content.

---

## What to Stop Doing

The existing content has a "bootcamp recaps" pattern (my-ux-ui-journey, figma-config, etc.) that doesn't serve SEO or either audience particularly well at this stage. Don't delete these — they may have some traffic — but don't write more like them. Future posts should always answer: "who is searching for this, or why would they share it?"
